# 📘 Sophos Firewall – Bandwidth Control (QoS) Configuration Guide

## 📖 Overview

**Bandwidth Control**, also known as **Bandwidth Capping** or **Quality of Service (QoS)**, is a feature in Sophos Firewall that helps administrators efficiently manage internet bandwidth across users, firewall rules, web traffic, and applications.

Organizations typically use one or more leased-line internet connections that are shared among multiple employees and devices. Without bandwidth management, a small number of users or background services can consume most of the available bandwidth, resulting in poor network performance for everyone.

---

# 🎯 Why Bandwidth Control Is Required

Consider the following scenario:

An employee reports that **Google Meet** or **Microsoft Teams** calls are experiencing:

* 📞 Voice interruptions
* 🎥 Poor video quality
* ⏳ Frequent lag or disconnections

Upon checking the firewall, you notice that the internet bandwidth is fully utilized.

Even after upgrading from:

* 100 Mbps to 200 Mbps
* Multiple leased-line connections

the issue continues to occur.

## ⚠️ Common Causes

Bandwidth is often consumed by activities such as:

* Windows Updates running simultaneously across multiple systems
* Antivirus signature updates
* Users streaming 4K YouTube videos
* Downloading large online courses
* Torrent (P2P) downloads
* Cloud synchronization applications

These activities can result in:

* Slow email access
* Web pages loading slowly
* Poor voice and video conferencing quality
* Overall network congestion

> **Example:**
> In an organization with 100 users, only **2–4 users** performing heavy downloads can significantly impact network performance.

To solve this problem, organizations implement **Quality of Service (QoS)**, also known as **Bandwidth Control** or **Bandwidth Capping**.

# 🛠 Where Can Traffic Shaping Policies Be Applied?

Bandwidth Control (QoS) can be applied in four different ways.

---

## 1️⃣ User-Based Traffic Shaping

Employees who authenticate using the **Captive Portal** can have individual bandwidth policies assigned.

Bandwidth control is configured directly in the user's account.

### Example

* Employee A → 10 Mbps
* Employee B → 5 Mbps
* Manager → 20 Mbps

---

## 2️⃣ Firewall Rule-Based Traffic Shaping

Some users or devices do not authenticate through the Captive Portal.

Examples include:

* Top management
* Servers
* Network devices

For these cases, apply bandwidth control directly to the firewall rule.

### Example

Suppose you have:

* 5 servers

You may want all servers to collectively use only **10 Mbps**.

This limitation is configured within the firewall rule.

---

## 3️⃣ Web Category Traffic Shaping

Bandwidth policies can also be applied to web traffic.

### Example

Large uploads or downloads from:

* Google Drive
* OneDrive

may consume significant bandwidth.

Traffic shaping allows you to limit bandwidth usage for these web categories.

---

## 4️⃣ Application-Based Traffic Shaping

Bandwidth can be managed based on specific applications.

### Examples

Limit bandwidth for:

* Dropbox
* File synchronization applications

Guarantee bandwidth for:

* Google Meet
* Microsoft Teams
* Zoom

This ensures business-critical communication applications always receive sufficient bandwidth.

# ⚙️ Rule Types

When creating a Traffic Shaping policy, choose one of the following rule types.

## 🔹 Limit

Defines the **maximum bandwidth** that users or devices are allowed to consume.

### Example

Employees are limited to:

* 5 Mbps
* 10 Mbps

They cannot exceed this configured limit.

---

## 🔹 Guarantee

Defines the **minimum bandwidth** reserved for users or applications.

### Example

* Directors receive a guaranteed **20 Mbps**
* Employees have a maximum limit of **5 Mbps** or **10 Mbps**

---

# ⬆️⬇️ Upload and Download Control

Traffic shaping policies allow you to configure:

* Shared upload/download bandwidth
* Separate upload and download limits

Choose the configuration based on your organization's requirements.

---

# 🚦 Traffic Priority

Sophos Firewall allows prioritization of network traffic.

| Priority | Description                   |
| -------- | ----------------------------- |
| **0**    | Real-Time (Highest Priority)  |
| **7**    | Best Effort (Lowest Priority) |

Assign priorities according to the importance of the traffic.

# 📝 Example Policy Creation

Create a Traffic Shaping policy for a user with a **10 Mbps** limit.

## Important Note

Traffic shaping values represent **download/upload bandwidth**, not browsing speed.

Actual file download speed is approximately:

```text
Download Speed = Bandwidth ÷ 8
```

### Example

```text
100 Mbps Internet Connection

100 ÷ 8

≈ 12.5 MB/s maximum download speed
```

For a **10 Mbps** limit:

```text
1024 × 10 ÷ 8 = 1280 Kbps
```

Configure:

```text
Limit = 1280 Kbps
```

---

# 📊 Bandwidth Type

Two bandwidth allocation methods are available.

## Shared

All users or devices share the configured bandwidth.

### Example

```text
20 Mbps Shared

PC1
PC2
PC3
PC4

Total = 20 Mbps
```

---

## Individual

Each device receives its own dedicated bandwidth allocation.

### Example

```text
PC1 = 10 Mbps
PC2 = 10 Mbps
PC3 = 10 Mbps
PC4 = 10 Mbps
```

Navigate to:

```text
Users
└── Select User
      └── Traffic Shaping
            └── Apply Policy
```

### Multiple Users

Instead of configuring users individually, create groups such as:

* IT
* HR
* Sales

Apply the Traffic Shaping policy to the group once.

---

# 🔥 Apply Policy to a Firewall Rule

Create a separate Traffic Shaping policy for firewall rules.

Navigate to:

```text
Rules & Policies
└── Firewall Rule
      └── Scroll Down
            └── Shape Traffic
```

> **Note:**
> Policies created for **Users** will **not** appear in Firewall Rules.

A dedicated Firewall Rule policy must be created.

---

# 🧪 Practical Example

Apply a **10 Mbps** Traffic Shaping policy to a firewall rule.

### Result

* Speed Test → Limited to **10 Mbps**

Remove the policy.

### Result

* Speed Test → Full internet speed is restored.
